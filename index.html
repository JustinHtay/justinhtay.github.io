What have I worked on?

In collarboration with Nathan Parrish and Dr. David V. Anderson, I worked on a project to generate normally-distributed random numbers. I implemented Nathan's modification of the ziggurat algorithm (originally proposed by Marsaglia and Tsang here) in both C and MATLAB, achieving results consistent with the predictions in his paper.

While at GTRI, I worked on a research project to extend GTRI's fragmented aperture antenna technology (detailed in this paper) from the traditional 2D space to the 3D space. The effort was extremely sucessful, and was recognized by the Chief Technical Officer (now Interim Director) of GTRI at a June 2017 presentation of internal research projects. While working on this project, I:

  • Used GTRI's in-house Finite Difference Time Domain (FDTD) codes to design and simulate the excitement of a 3D fragmented aperture antenna.
  • Used a genetic algorithm to optimize the antenna design over hundreds of thousands of iterations.
  • Wrote an algorithm to ensure connectivity of all the pieces of the antenna so that it could be manufactured. The algorithm, written in Python, used the properties of an adjacency matrix from graph theory to run in feasible time, whereas the previous DFS algorithm I implemented was infeasible.
My final designs were successfully printed and measured. They had good measure-to-model accuracy, and achieved the desired gain over a wide band (6-18 GHz). These findings were ultimately written up into a report and then a paper that is currently being prepared for publication.
