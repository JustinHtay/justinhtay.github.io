Engineer. Mathematician. Teacher. Leader.

Who am I?

That's a good question! My name is Justin Htay. I'm a third-year double major in Electrical Engineering and Mathematics at the Georgia Institute of Technology. I specialize in bringing together the theoretical and the practical. I combine a strong background in mathematics, especially in combinatorics and probability, with a wealth of experience in coding, design, and electrical engineering fundamentals. I've worked extensively with Linux systems, bash scripting, and Python coding. I'm a passionate teacher and work as an undergraduate teaching assistant with the College of Computing at Georgia Tech, teaching an introduction to programming course in MATLAB.

In my free time, you'll find that I have a love for history, and can often be found nose-deep in a history book or simulating it in a video game. I keep my wits sharp with a game of chess and my body healthy with a long bike ride. Music is a big part of my life and I'm most relaxed on a piano bench or singing in Chorale. I carry on my cultural heritage through a love of cooking and food.
