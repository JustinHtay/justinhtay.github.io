Engineer. Mathematician. Teacher. Leader.

Who am I?

That's a good question! My name is Justin Htay. I'm a third-year double major in Electrical Engineering and Mathematics at the Georgia Institute of Technology. I specialize in bringing together the theoretical and the practical. I combine a strong background in mathematics, especially in combinatorics and probability, with a wealth of experience in coding, design, and electrical engineering fundamentals. I've worked extensively with Linux systems, bash scripting, and Python coding. I'm a passionate teacher and work as an undergraduate teaching assistant with the College of Computing at Georgia Tech, teaching an introduction to programming course in MATLAB.

In my free time, you'll find that I have a love for history, and can often be found nose-deep in a history book or simulating it in a video game. I keep my wits sharp with a game of chess and my body healthy with a long bike ride. Music is a big part of my life and I'm most relaxed on a piano bench or singing in Chorale. I carry on my cultural heritage through a love of cooking and food.

Where have I worked?

I currently work as an Undergraduate Teaching Assistant (UTA) for the College of Computing at Georgia Tech, teaching CS 1371, which is the introductory programming class for most engineering students at Georgia Tech. We have over 1000 students enrolled in the class in the larger semesters. As a TA, I have:

  • Served as Course Manager and Homework Lead concurrently for two summer semesters. As Course Manager, I was responsible for smooth operation of the class, managing the TA team, and communicating with the professor who taught the class. As Homework Lead, I published weekly homeworks for the class.
  • Wrote 20+ homework problems that were both creative and instructive for students.
  • Taught 1.5-2 hour recitations and held 3 hours of office hours weekly for a section of approximately 50 students.
I have worked as a TA from January 2017 until the present.

I worked as a High-Performance Computing (HPC) co-op in the Advanced Concepts Laboratory (ACL) of the Georgia Tech Research Institute (GTRI). I was responsible for maintaining and upgrading 10+ HPC resources and performed numerous electromagnetic modeling tasks. Highlights of my time at GTRI include:

  • Applying numerical optimization techniques (e.g genetic algorithms) to design electromagnetic structures using the FDTD method
  • Designing a fragmented aperture antenna that was produced using a novel 3D-printed approach that resulted in a paper.
  • Designing a Frequency Selective Surface (FSS) for use in a novel periodic structure. My designs were important in getting the project extended a year with additional funding.
  • Installing 5 major ACL computing resources on Linux operating systems (both RHEL and Ubuntu) and managing 5+ other resources, extensively utilizing my knowledge of Linux, bash, and problem-solving techniques.
  • Creating a dashboard using Grafana to display ACL achievements and user statistics on the clustering resources to prospective customers.
I worked at GTRI from January 2017 to August 2018.

The National History Bee and Bowl (NHBB) organizes quiz competitions for middle and high school students around the globe on primarily historical content. Thanks to my experience in Academic Bowl and love of history, I am a seasoned writer of questions. At NHBB, I:

  • Wrote 800+ questions with a 99% acceptance rate. These questions were used in all levels of competition, from local tournaments to the International History Olympiad.
  • Wrote 40%+ of a set on short notice, which is a very high proportion for a single writer. For my efforts, I was recognized by the Director for Question Production for outstanding contributions.
